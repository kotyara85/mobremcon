So why did we created SLITE?

It all started with me, CEO, being a DJ. Yes a DJ in nightclubs. After I was already an established DJ, I started producing my own events. What I liked about it was that this was an infinite medium: you could do fashion shows, paintings, masquerades and so on. Over time my events grew from simple DJ dancing to extravagant shows. But even that was not enough. I was limited by the walls of the clubs where I was doing my shows. And then I looked into the web …

Who likes trivia shows? I do. So I hang out with very very smart people: PhDs, scientists, and just simply walking Wikipedias (and models due to my DJ career). And with the best of them we formed our company - SLITE.

We simply got tired of all the limitation of the PowerPoint: being forced to squeeze data into slides, waste people's time with projector setups, and not be able to do what we used to with other technologies: how many times you tried to "like" a slide? Just to tell you how bad this problem is: it was determined that Shuttle Discovery disaster happened because of the PowerPoint inherit notion of data condensation. Did I mention that with SLITE you can have infinite! data per slide? People cannot deal anymore with something that was designed in 1995 to model after the film which you used to stick into a projector. One of our missions in SLITE is to change the English meaning of the word “presentation”.
